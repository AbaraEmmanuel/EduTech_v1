📝 How to write a great profile description ▼
  1. Introduce yourself — Your name (first only), where you're from, and why you love teaching.
  2. Your experience — Years taught, types of students, exam boards covered (WAEC, JAMB, IELTS…).
  3. Teaching style — Are you patient? Structured? Practical? Tell students what makes you different.
  4. Call to action — End with an encouraging message inviting students to book a trial lesson.
❌ Do NOT include: Phone numbers, emails, WhatsApp handles, social media links, or your last name.

Introduction Video

Tutors with intro videos get 3× more bookings. Keep it natural and 1–2 minutes long.

🎥

Click to upload your intro video

MP4, MOV — Max 100MB — 1–3 minutes

✅ DO

  • Record in a quiet, well-lit room
  • Speak clearly and naturally — avoid scripts
  • Show your personality and enthusiasm
  • Mention your subject, experience & teaching style
  • End with a warm invitation to book a lesson

❌ DON'T

  • Share phone numbers or social media handles
  • Mention your last name
  • Read from a script word-for-word
  • Use a shaky or blurry camera
  • Have loud background noise or music
💡
Script guide: Try saying — "Hi! I'm [First Name], a [subject] tutor with [X] years of experience. I love helping students [specific goal]. My lessons are [style description]. I'd love to help you — let's book a trial session!"
